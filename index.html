What we do

We support the creation and growth of more innovative herbal and natural products, organic agriculture, bioinformatics, technology- based and ICT-related companies in the country with focus on startup software, content, hardware, and solution provider enterprises whose business models incorporate open technologies.


Services Offered

  • Security and Maintenance
  • Shared Meeting, Training, and Conferencing Facilities
  • High-speed Internet Connection and Data Center Services
  • Business and Professional Assistance
  • Support Services
  • Business/Professional/Technical Consultancy Services
  • Mentoring and Coaching
  • Technology Forecasting and Matching
  • Laboratory and Production
  • R&D Funding Assistance and other Financing Assistance

Services offered for resident incubatees include physical office spaces constructed from low-cost and rapid setup 3 stories. The smallest leasable unit shall have a usable interior of twenty (20) m2. The project will also be offering in the near future virtual incubation services for non-resident incubatees.

Message from the WVSU President

Dear Friends!

"Without change, there is no innovation, creativity or incentive for improvement. Those who initiate change will have a better opportunity to manage the change that is inevitable." (William Pollard)

Iloilo nowadays, with its current booming and fast changing economy is in need of continuing support from its economic community for the maintenance and further development of its industry. More than ever, the creation of the GTBI Facility inside WVSU campus is a good opportunity for innovating the enterprises of Iloilo and of Western Visayas as a whole, as West Visayas State University is at the forefront of green technology because of its R&D on herbal pharmaceuticals, organic agriculture, bioinformatics and information and communications technology.

Green Technology Business Incubation is a program designed to support, accelerate and nurture the successful development of entrepreneurial companies. Equipped with its sophisticated programs to increase the potential growth and development of its Incubatees, the facility will serve as a catalyst for the furtherance and innovation of business enterprise and the whole business economy in Western Visayas.

Planned and built from scratch, the GTBI Facility is created through the foresight, imagination, hard work, and cooperation of a number of partnering agencies namely, Department of Science and Technology(DOST) through its branch, the Philippine Council for Industry, Energy and Emerging Technology Research and Development (PCIEERD), Technology Resource Center (TRC) and the Commission on Higher Education (CHED). Through this, the GTBI Facility and its Management is ready for their start up, and offer significant services for the production of independent and competitive enterprises which will be at the forefront of business society in the days to come.

So enjoy this new facility, visit its website, learn more about its services. All of its benefits are available for use... Take advantage!

History of GTBI

The DOST-Technology Resource Center (TRC) and the idea of technology business incubation (TBI) was first made known to WVSU through Ms. Cheryl L. Ortega, formerly training officer of the Development Academy of the Philippines and became Development Officer III of TRC and after a series of DOST visitations.

History of GTBI - 2008

An exploratory visit of TRC upon the approval of TRC Director Antonio Y. Ortiz, in May 28, 2008 upon the request of the then University Research and Development Center Director Gerard L. Penecilla through Mr. Abelardo Valdez, TRC Technology Assessment and Commercialization Department Manager, made the possibility of commercializing various WVSU R&D products including the "Darag" native chicken, antibacterial natural products and new software development/ICT packaging with the efforts of Dr. Joel T. de Castro.

2008

WVSU R&D started to present its research products in many Technology Fora and Scientific Fair sponsored by DOST-TRC in Manila, Pampanga, and Cebu (ca 2009-2012).

2009

The first MOA between WVSU and TRC under Director-General Dennis L. Cunanan was executed in January 2010 in order to make a feasibility study on the establishment of a Green Technology Business Incubation Facility (GTBI) in WVSU.

History of GTBI - 2010

The feasibility study was completed on the same year through the financial support of DOST-PCASTRD (Philippine Council for Advanced Science and Technology Research and Development) now known as PCIEERD or the Philippine Council for Industry, Energy and Emerging Technologies Research and Development. With about 38 incubatee-respondents, a Business Plan on the GTBI was made through the technical support of TRC and the all-out support of then President Pablo E. Subong, Jr. We were then on the process of finding financial support for the construction of the building.

2010

In March 10, 2011, the GTBI at WVSU was given the lot near Magsaysay Road for the initial ground breaking ceremonies attended by TRC, DTI, DOST, Iloilo City Government and CHED officials.

History of GTBI - 2011

IT Research Presentation Represented by IICT Students, won the 2nd , 3rd and 4 th place respectively. February 11, 2011. Mega Trade Hall 3 at SM Mega Mall Quezon City.

In this year, a meeting was also held at Guimaras.

This was also the time where the ground breaking ceremony for the GTBI building was initiated.

2011

The series of events paved the way for the initial funding of 17 million pesos in July 2012 from CHED through Chairman Patricia B. Licuanan and Dr. Pablo E Subong, Jr..

History of GTBI - 2012

A Meeting was also held this year.

2012

The construction of the Building started in 2013 with the support of OIC-President Joselito F. Villaruz, MD, VPFA Bobby Gerardo, and IICT Director Joel T. De Castro as well as the proposal for PCIEERD for the establishment of the Natural Products Business Incubation Facility in WVSU.

History of GTBI - 2013

The first steering committee meeting attended by the Iloilo City Government, DTI, DOST, TRC and CHED was done in July 16, 2013 to approve the operation manual. Dr. Rowena Cristina L. Guevara, Executive Director of DOST PCIEERD with the able support of Ms. Russell M. Pili, made possible the funding of 8.6 million pesos for the equipment and operation cost for two years (2014-2016) of the facility . Dr. Luis M. Sorolla, current WVSU President made possible the WVSU counterpart of more than six million pesos for additional facilities and equipment and completion of the four-storey building. Thus the NPBI will officially start on December 16, 2014 with five incubatees.
